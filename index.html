AI for Healthcare

The Mayo Clinic Artificial Intelligence Summit 2023 will bring together researchers from Mayo Clinic and external experts to learn about current AI activities in the healthcare domain and the rich varied possibilities of AI for healthcare.

Register

Cure. Connect. Transform.

Healthcare is an area with great opportunity for Artificial Intelligence. The vast volumes of machine-processable data generated in healthcare both enable and require the application of modern advanced AI methodologies.

Register

AI + Clinical Data

The large number of news stories and reports of AI in the healthcare domain has created an environment filled with inflated expectations, baseless fears, and mismatches between use cases and technologies.

Register

Previous Next

Mayo Clinic, driven by its values, adopts a future-forward approach to leading health care transformation. Leveraging medical excellence and digital health sciences, artificial intelligence plays a critical role in supporting the Mayo 2030 strategy in achieving the mission of curing and preventing diseases, connecting people everywhere, and transforming healthcare through platforms. The Mayo Clinic Artificial Intelligence Summit aims to bring the health care AI community together to learn about current activities, share best practices, and foster collaborations toward digital health and medicine.

CALL FOR PARTICIPATION

Call for Abstracts

We invite the submission of abstracts within the scope of the AI Summit for poster and short oral presentations. Presentations should focus on AI for healthcare and should be targeted at a mixed audience of stakeholders including researchers, clinical practitioners, and senior decision-makers. Abstracts should be submitted by January 31, 2023.

Call for Breakout Sessions

At the 2023 AI Summit, we can host several special breakout sessions. We are calling for proposals for these breakout sessions. Your proposal should include the information below and be emailed to aisummit@mayo.edu:

  • Organizers: Names, affiliations, and email addresses; contact person; organizers' background.
  • Topic: Abstract, theme, rationale for the breakout sessions, issues to be addressed, goals of the sessions, relevance to the conference (500-1,000 words)
  • Audience: Target audience, communities that the workshop addresses, anticipated number of attendees.
  • Structure: The sessions will not overlap with the keynote/panel sessions. It can be 60 minutes, 90 minutes, or 120 minutes. The format can be workshops, panels, data challenges, hackathons, tutorials, special sessions digging in depth of a topic area.
  • Reviewing: The proposal will be reviewed by the steering committee with decisions made within two weeks
All accepted proposals will be reviewed by the steering committee and confirmed or declined within two weeks of submission. Deadline for submission: January 31, 2023
